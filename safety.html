Safety

Our Commitment

Safety Is Our Promise

At N Line Electric, safety is not a policy—it's a core value. We work on medium voltage and industrial power systems where the stakes are high. Every job is planned, executed, and reviewed with the same focus: send everyone home safe and keep your facility and the public protected.

Our teams are trained, equipped, and empowered to stop work when conditions aren't right. We maintain a culture where speaking up about safety is expected and respected—from the job site to the office.

Culture

Our Safety Culture

We have developed a culture of safety at N Line Electric. From daily tailgate meetings and hazard discussions to near-miss reporting and lessons learned, we treat safety as a shared responsibility. Our field and office staff are trained to recognize and control hazards before work begins and to communicate clearly with clients and other trades when we're on your site.

Safety is built into how we bid work, schedule crews, and select equipment—so we never compromise on time or budget at the expense of doing the job safely.

Readiness

Training & PPE

We continuously train our staff in the latest safety standards and best practices for medium voltage and industrial electrical work. Topics include arc flash and shock prevention, lockout/tagout (LOTO), confined space awareness, fall protection, and first aid/CPR where required. We also provide our workers with appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE)—including arc-rated clothing, voltage-rated gloves and tools, and hard hats and eye protection—so they are properly equipped for the hazards they face.

  • Arc flash and electrical safety training
  • Lockout/tagout (LOTO) procedures
  • PPE: arc-rated FR clothing, voltage-rated gloves and tools
  • Job briefings and tailgate meetings
  • Client and site-specific safety orientations
On the Job

Jobsite Practices

We post signs and barricades where we work so your personnel and the public know to stay clear. Our crews follow established work plans, use proper grounding and testing procedures on medium voltage systems, and coordinate with your team when shutdowns or switching are required. We document our safety and quality steps so you have a clear record of how the work was performed.

If you have site-specific rules, inductions, or permit requirements, we comply with them and factor them into our scheduling and execution.

Accountability

Emergency Response & Accountability

We plan for the unexpected. Our teams know how to respond to incidents, report injuries or near-misses, and escalate issues when needed. We work with clients to align emergency procedures and contacts so that if something goes wrong, everyone knows what to do. We also hold ourselves accountable: we track leading and lagging safety indicators and use that data to improve our programs and share lessons learned.

Questions?

We Post Signs Where We Work

When we're on your site, you'll see our signage and barricades. If you're not sure what a sign means or how to stay safe around our work area, ask our crew or contact us. We're happy to explain our procedures and how we're keeping your facility and people safe.

For safety documentation, site-specific safety requirements, or to discuss how we can support your project safely, get in touch.
